The Vibe

Juárez is defined by The Phoenix. A former aristocratic glory rising from the ashes to become the edgiest neighborhood.

Landmarks

  • The Angel of Independence, Chocolate Museum.

Living & Building in Juárez

Juárez is eclectic. You have the Korean community, heavy metal bars, and ultra-luxury hotels all in one zone. It feels urban, gritty, and exciting. It attracts a crowd that finds Roma too polished. It is the home of 'Little Tokyo' and the Korean community, giving it a unique international flavor. The vibe is raw, urban, and exciting. It feels like a neighborhood on the verge of superstardom. You can find the best Korean BBQ in the city next to a 100-year-old French mansion that is being converted into a co-working space. It borders the financial district on Reforma, making it incredibly convenient for young professionals.

Service Perspective: Designing a home in Juárez means creating a sanctuary. The density of the neighborhood requires creative solutions for privacy and acoustics. We focus on internal courtyards and sound-insulated glazing to create peace within the urban energy.

A History of Design

In the late 1800s, this was the neighborhood for the wealthy. It was abandoned in the mid-century and became the 'Zona Rosa'. Today, it is being restored. The architecture is a fascinating mix of Porfirian mansions (many in disrepair but beautiful) and mid-century apartment blocks. The 'Zona Rosa' section became famous as a nightlife and LGBTQ+ hub in the 70s and 80s.

The Investment Outlook

Aggressive growth. Prices have doubled in 5 years but are still lower than Roma. Best for appreciation. It is currently the highest-ROI zone for renovation projects. The location is unbeatable—bordering both the historic center and the modern business district. As gentrification spills over from Roma, Juárez is the direct beneficiary.

Market Insight: Residential properties in Juárez are the most resilient asset class. Families prioritize this zone for its walkability and parks, ensuring that a well-designed home here will always have a buyer.

Insider Tip: Boutique Hotels and Airbnbs are the killer app here due to proximity to Reforma.

The Challenge

For residential projects, the main hurdle is often maximizing square footage within the strict 'Area Libre' (Free Area) requirements of the zoning code.
Furthermore, structural risk. many 1900s mansions were damaged in earthquakes.

Our Solution

Our residential team is expert at utilizing roof terraces and light wells to count towards open space requirements while expanding the usable living area. We partner with top structural engineers to ensure every renovation meets 2026 seismic codes.

Led by Erik Martínez (AIA, NCARB), formerly of SHoP Architects NYC, we navigate these complexities with precision.
