Living & Building in Lomas de Chapultepec

Lomas is not a 'neighborhood' in the social sense; it is a collection of private compounds. It is car-dependent, quiet, and lushly green. The lifestyle is one of ultimate privacy and luxury. It attracts diplomats and captains of industry. Life happens behind the walls or at exclusive clubs like the Club de Golf Chapultepec. It offers the most square footage and privacy of any central zone. The streets are wide, winding, and lined with massive trees, giving it a suburban feel despite being minutes from the business district. It is the only place in the city where you can have a sprawling garden, a swimming pool, and a tennis court on your own property.

A History of Design

Established in the 1920s as 'Chapultepec Heights,' it was designed by British/American planners to mimic the 'Garden City' movement with winding streets. The intent was to create an exclusive enclave separated from the chaos of the city center. Over the decades, it has remained the address of choice for Mexico's political and economic elite. The architecture ranges from massive neo-colonial mansions to ultra-modern concrete masterpieces designed by the country's top architects.

The Investment Outlook

A fortress of value. Lomas real estate is virtually immune to market crashes. It holds value in USD terms better than any other zone. It is a generational asset class. While rental yields are lower (percentage-wise) due to the high asset value, the security of the principal is unmatched. It is where you park wealth to preserve it.

Insider Tip: Security is the ultimate amenity. Investing in a 'Safe Room' and perimeter technology adds dollar-for-dollar value.
