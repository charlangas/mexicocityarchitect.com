Living & Building in Del Valle

Del Valle is the '15-minute city' realized. Everything you need is within walking distance. It is less pretentious than Polanco and less chaotic than Roma. It's the preferred choice for families who want safety and space without the Lomas price tag. It boasts some of the best parks in the city (Parque de los Venados, Parque Arboledas) which are always full of children and dogs. The housing stock is mostly vertical—high-quality apartments built in the 70s, 80s, and 90s. It is not 'trendy' in the Instagram sense, but it is incredibly functional. It has excellent public transport connectivity and is generally considered one of the safest zones in the central city.

Service Perspective: Interiors in Del Valle demand a dialogue with the exterior. We bring the colors and textures of the neighborhood—volcanic stone, jacaranda purple, terracotta—inside to create a sense of place.

A History of Design

Developed in the mid-20th century, it has a mix of functionalist houses and 1980s apartment blocks. It was originally a zone of country houses and orchards, which explains the width of its avenues and the abundance of trees. It experienced rapid verticalization in the late 20th century, becoming the quintessential middle-class neighborhood of CDMX.

The Investment Outlook

High liquidity. Del Valle apartments sell and rent very fast. It is a volume game. Because it is so central and livable, there is a constant stream of demand from professionals and young families. It is a low-risk, medium-return market. The opportunity lies in renovation—buying tired 1980s apartments and updating them for the modern market.

Market Insight: High-end interior design is the differentiator in Del Valle's competitive rental market. A unit with bespoke joinery and lighting rents for 30-40% more than a standard white-box apartment.

Insider Tip: The 'Flipping' Sweet Spot. Buy a dated 1990s apartment and do a cosmetic update for a reliable margin.

The Challenge

The challenge is often lighting. Older buildings in Del Valle can be deep and dark. Bringing light into the core of the floorplate is our primary design objective.
Furthermore, construction logistics. most projects are in active apartment buildings.

Our Solution

We use reflective materials, strategic mirrors, and warm 2700K lighting schemes to make even ground-floor apartments feel airy and luminous. We excel at 'Neighbor Diplomacy' to minimize friction with HOAs.

Led by Erik Martínez (AIA, NCARB), formerly of SHoP Architects NYC, we navigate these complexities with precision.
