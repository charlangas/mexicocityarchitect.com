Living & Building in Roma Sur

If Roma Norte is for partying, Roma Sur is for living. It feels like the Mexico City of 20 years ago—traditional, neighborly, and slow-paced. It is quieter, greener, and significantly more residential. The lifestyle is about local markets (Mercado de Medellín) and home cooking. Residents here are a mix of original families who have lived here for generations and young professionals seeking the Roma location without the Norte noise. You will find 'Tienditas' (corner stores) run by the same families for decades. It is becoming the refuge for those who love the aesthetic of the area but prioritize sleep and community over nightlife. The connectivity is excellent, with easy access to the Metrobus and major avenues, making it a practical choice for commuters.

Service Perspective: Renovating in Roma Sur is an act of archaeology. Every wall we open reveals layers of history—sometimes beautiful (original frescoes), sometimes challenging (old plumbing). We love the process of restoring these layers.

A History of Design

Developed slightly later than the Norte, Roma Sur features a mix of Art Deco, Californian Colonial, and Mid-Century Functionalist architecture. It was traditionally a middle-class stronghold and has resisted the extreme commercialization of its northern neighbor. The urban layout is similar—a grid of tree-lined streets—but the density is lower, with more single-family homes remaining intact rather than being converted into apartment blocks.

The Investment Outlook

Roma Sur is the 'Value Play.' Prices are 20-30% lower than Norte, but the appreciation curve is steeper as demand spills over. It is poised for significant growth as the next logical step for gentrification. Investors who buy here are betting on the inevitable southward expansion of the 'cool' zone. It offers a better entry point for those priced out of Norte or Condesa, with arguably higher upside potential in the next 5-10 years.

Market Insight: Renovation offers the highest ROI in Roma Sur. Buying a 'fixer-upper' and updating systems/finishes generates significantly more equity than buying new construction.

Insider Tip: Buy single-family homes here. Adding a third story (studio/roof garden) is often permitted and creates massive equity.

The Challenge

The logistics of renovation in Roma Sur are tough. Narrow streets make debris removal difficult, and noise restrictions are strict in these dense residential zones.
Furthermore, lot sizes are often smaller and layouts in 1950s houses can be compartmentalized and dark.

Our Solution

Our construction teams are trained in 'Low-Impact Renovation.' We use specialized smaller trucks for debris and strictly adhere to work hours to maintain neighborly relations. Our design team specializes in 'Light Surgery'—strategic demolition of internal walls and adding skylights.

Led by Erik Martínez (AIA, NCARB), formerly of SHoP Architects NYC, we navigate these complexities with precision.
